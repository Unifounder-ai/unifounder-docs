---
title: "LinkedIn"
description: "Get a LinkedIn Marketing access token plus your company-page URN so Unifounder can publish company posts and read post and page metrics."
---

Unifounder publishes to LinkedIn through the Marketing Developer Platform. You give it two things: an OAuth 2.0 access token with the right scopes, and the URN of the company page you want to post on behalf of.

The whole setup takes about 15 minutes once your LinkedIn developer app is approved. App approval can take a few business days, so start there first.

## Access token

The token must be an OAuth 2.0 access token with two scopes:

- `w_organization_social` — publish posts.
- `r_organization_social` — read post and page metrics.

### Step 1 — Create or pick a LinkedIn developer app

1. Go to [linkedin.com/developers/apps](https://www.linkedin.com/developers/apps) and sign in with the LinkedIn account that is a page admin of the company you want to post on.
2. Click "Create app" or pick an existing one.
3. Associate the app with your company page. You must be an admin of that page for this to work.

### Step 2 — Request Marketing Developer Platform access

1. Open your app and go to the "Products" tab.
2. Find "Marketing Developer Platform" and click "Request access". Fill out the use-case form. Something like "automated company-page publishing for our internal tooling" is fine.
3. Wait for approval. This is the slow part — usually 1 to 3 business days. You will get an email when it lands.

<Note>
  Until LinkedIn approves your app, the `w_organization_social` scope is not available and Unifounder will reject the token with a `permission_denied` error.
</Note>

### Step 3 — Generate an access token

1. In your app, open the "Auth" tab and copy your Client ID and Client Secret.
2. Open LinkedIn's OAuth 2.0 token generator at [linkedin.com/developers/tools/oauth/token-generator](https://www.linkedin.com/developers/tools/oauth/token-generator).
3. Pick your app, check both scopes (`w_organization_social` and `r_organization_social`), and click "Request access token".
4. Sign in with your LinkedIn account when prompted, and copy the access token LinkedIn returns.

<Tip>
  Tokens generated this way are valid for 60 days. Unifounder warns you in the connectors panel before expiry. To rotate, repeat Step 3 and re-paste.
</Tip>

### Step 4 — Paste into Unifounder

In the "Connect LinkedIn" modal, paste the token into the "Access token" field. Then continue to "Organization URN" below.

## Organization URN

The URN identifies the company page Unifounder should post on behalf of. It looks like `urn:li:organization:1234567`.

### How to find it

1. Open your company page on LinkedIn at `linkedin.com/company/<your-page>`.
2. While signed in as a page admin, switch to the Admin view.
3. Look at the URL. You will see `linkedin.com/company/<page>/admin/dashboard/`. The numeric id in the page slug or in the page-id query string is your organization id.
4. Prefix it with `urn:li:organization:`. For example, `urn:li:organization:1234567`.

<Tip>
  Unifounder accepts the bare numeric id too. Paste `1234567` and it will prefix the URN for you.
</Tip>

### Verify the page-admin link

Unifounder's `linkedin.publish_company_post` only works if the token's user is an admin of the page identified by the URN. If they do not match, LinkedIn returns `permission_denied` and the post step fails.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="expired_token">
    Token is past 60 days. Repeat Step 3 above and re-paste in the connectors panel.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="permission_denied on publish">
    Your LinkedIn account is not an admin of the company page, or the Marketing Developer Platform request was denied. Re-check Step 1 and Step 2.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="rate_limited">
    LinkedIn throttles publishes per app and per page. Unifounder backs off automatically. The retry surfaces in the mission timeline.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="scope_missing">
    Token does not have `w_organization_social` or `r_organization_social`. Re-generate with the correct scopes checked in Step 3.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What Unifounder reads vs writes

| Capability | Tool slug | Scope |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Publish a company post | `linkedin.publish_company_post` | `w_organization_social` |
| Read post metrics (impressions, likes, comments, shares, clicks) | background | `r_organization_social` |
| Read page metrics (followers, profile views, weekly impressions) | background | `r_organization_social` |

Unifounder never reads or writes your personal feed. Only the company page identified by the URN.
